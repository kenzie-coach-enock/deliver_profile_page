05/25/2023: NASA combines images from the Chandra and Webb telescopes for stunning views ❱

Four composite images from the Chandra X-ray Observatory and the James Webb Space Telescope provide stunning views of two galaxies, a nebula, and a star cluster. Each image combines Chandra's X-rays— a form of high-energy light— with infrared data from previously released Webb images, both invisible to the naked eye. Data from NASA's Hubble Space Telescope (optical light) and the now-retired Spitzer Space Telescope (infrared), along with XMM-Newton (X-rays) from the European Space Agency and the New Technology Telescope of the European Southern Observatory (optical) were also used. These cosmic wonders and details are made available by mapping the data and transforming it into colors that the human eye can perceive.
